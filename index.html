About

Abigail Aiken is a postdoctoral fellow at the Office of Population Research at Princeton University. Her research concerns reproductive health and spans several disciplines, combining her background in biomedical science with training in demography, public health, and public policy. Her articles have been published in The American Journal of Public Health, Perspectives and Sexual and Reproductive Health, Obstretrics and Gynecology, the American Journal of Obstetrics and Gynecology, among others. Her research has been supported by the National Institutes of Health and the Society for Family Planning.

Abigail grew up in Northern Ireland and graduated from the University of Cambridge with her bachelor's and medical degrees. She then earned her PhD in Public Affairs from the University of Texas at Austin, where she studied at the LBJ School and the Population Research Center.
